This is a very common webpage example that has a fixed header, fixed footer and a big scrollable content in the middle. This project aims to demonstrate the layer repaint efficiency by creating layers for each part of the website. By having different layers, if one of them changes, we won't repaint the whole page again. To see the layers created by your browser you can use Chrome (I'm actually on version 114) DevTools. You can open DevTools and then click on the three dots for more options at the top rightmost position on the DevTools, on the right of the closing/X button.

You'll see a menu popping up, move your mouse over More Tools and then look for Layers on the new pop up that just opened. Click on Layers and you'll see a new tab on your DevTools. You'll then see three button right under the DevTools tabs. Click on the second one and then click and drag the page preview being displayed on the Layers tab. You'll be able to rotate the page and see the distance between the base page and the layers that were created that should be floating.

Also, if you would like, you can use Chrome to highlight every repaint in your webpage. Just go to the DevTools > Three Dots > More tools > Rendering and then check the Paint flashing checkbox. You can now scroll the webpage and see the browser repainting the page as little as possible.
